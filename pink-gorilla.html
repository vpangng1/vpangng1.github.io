An unsolicited redesign of the Pink Gorilla Game's website.

Project Details

My Role: Individual Project

Duration: 2 Weeks

Tools: Sketch, InVision, Figma, Lucidchart, Optimal Sort, Pencil & Paper

Background

Pink Gorilla Games is a Seattle-based retro and imported video game retailer that sells video games, books, clothing, collectibles, toys, edibles, and unique items. Inspired by Japanese game stores, PGG aims to be colorful, nostalgic, and fresh.

Problem

PGG proclaims to be the "coolest and fastest growing video game store in the world", but users have a tough time navigating the website and getting a sense of what products are in store.

Solution

Creating a simple and organized way for users to see all online and in-store items while still having access to information about the nostalgic and vintage in-store experience.

Research & Discovery

Using a variety of methods including interviews, viewing reviews, and affinity mapping, I was able to extract key business and customer needs. These needs helped me understand about the problems and how I would approach them.

Interviews

To kick off user discovery, I conducted interviews with five customers to learn about their motivations and reasons for coming to the store. Two customers I spoke up with said that they came to the store after hearing good things from reviews and customers. Another customer enjoyed the friendly staff and atmosphere which is unlike any other game store. Customers described the store as being very clean, colorful, and organized.

Reviews

Based on reviews from Google and Yelp, I found that in addition to finding obscure items, customers enjoy coming to the store for the fun and nostalgic experience. However, customers wished that the store was more organized and displayed better.

Values & Goals

From my research and findings, I mapped out the customer values and business goals. Customers of PGG want a cool & fun experience as well as being able to learn more about the business. Business goals of the organization include inviting new customers while appealing to older generation of gamers.

Persona

For the purposes of this project, I created two personas based on research and findings. Tiffany and John represent the younger and older generation gamer respectively. Tiffany is someone who needs relationship with the brand and keeps up with information through social media and reviews. On the other hand, John Goodson needs trusting and intuitive services that help him navigate products and items.

Define & Strategy

Before beginning the design process, I needed to first understand the full scope of the site redesign. To accomplish this, I researched PGG's existing site content and compared it with a few competitor websites.

Competitve Analysis

Content comparison helped me see the most common web content for video game stores such as game categories, about, reviews, news, etc. This exploration also helped me notice common naming and category organization conventions. I discovered that PGG's website lacks information about their business and that most of it can be found from outside sources.

Existing Sitemap

I mapped out the current site map of PGG's website. The existing site is disorganized, redundant, and misleading, making it difficult to navigate.

Card Sorting

I conducted a card sorting activity using Optimal Sort to see how users would organize the content and categories of the current website.

Design Approach

The redesign is simplified for ease of searching and clarity. Content categories reflect common convention and user needs.

New Sitemap

I created a new sitemap based on the results of the card sorting activity. The About section contains information about the business and the team to help establish trust. News and Events inform people about what's new & cool to encourage new and existing customers to come check out the store.

Homepage

To increase visibility of all the available categories based on the site map, I placed the navigation categories (about, location, news, events) in the top right corner. Product categories were placed near the top center for easy and convenient access for the user regardless of page location.
