Basketball Manual

Step by step instructions on proper shooting form.

ABOUT BASKETBALL MANUAL

The basketball manual was created using Adobe Illustrator as part of a final project at Curry College. The purpose of this project was to design professional looking illustrations seen in instruction manuals using vector graphics.

I started this project by taking a bunch of pictures of my brother in different poses holding the basketball. In this picture, you can see that his eyes are focused on the hoop and his right hand palming the center of the ball.

I then translated the picture into a vector graphic using Illustrator. I traced the right hand using the pen tool and color coded it to emphasize that hand positioning was important when shooting a basketball.

In addition to hand form, I wanted to focus on leg form as well. In this image, my brother is bending his knees slightly in order to get ready for a jump shot. I highlighted the knees to show that proper shooting form requires both arms and legs.

In this illustration, my brother is raising his hands up and cuffs the ball with his palm. He is also bending his knees down further so he can prepare himself for a jump. Here I was trying to stress that proper form was important for shooting consistent and accurate jump shots.

I created the up arrow in this illustration to demonstrate the act of jumping. I color coded the whole leg to show that the act of jump shooting requires the entire leg, from extending the leg straight to pointing your toes down. Note that he is holding the same arm position from the previous step.

Here in the final step, I tried to illustrate that once my brother is at the peak of his jump, he releases the basketball from his right hand and travels from the tip of his finger.
