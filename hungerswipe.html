Project Details

My Role: Visual & Interaction Designer

Duration: 7 Months - Ongoing

Tools: Figma, Illustrator

Background

HungerSwipe is a mobile application that helps people decide on a place to eat more efficiently. HungerSwipe does this by adopting the swipe operation model seen in popular apps like Tinder. As the visual and interaction designer, I was tasked with coming up with designs to integrate the swipe feature with crowd-sourced local restaurant review & location apps such as Yelp.

Research Insights

Our model user Sierra Miller is a college student who is invested in social media apps and enjoys sharing her experiences with friends. Our research team team also discovered that all our interviewees at least one instance of fighting with friends over where to eat.

Problem

When it comes to choosing a place to eat there are factors that have to be taken into consideration such as maximum travel distance, price range, type of food, etc. Deciding which place to eat with one or more people can become a frustrating experience that can lead to indecision and arguments because their preferences also have to be taken into account.

Solution

We believe implementing a feature that allows users to swipe on desired restaurants makes it easier for parties to decide where to eat. We will know when we're right when we see an increase in downloads, satisfaction, and ratings.

Design Ideation & Sketches

Early concept sketches of the HungerSwipe app had Tinder's interface in mind. This was done to increase simplicity and ease for our target users who are already familiar with apps such as Tinder.

Usability Testing & Findings

Our research team conducted three rounds of usability testings. The first round of prototyping was conducted with pen & paper and the second and third rounds were done within Figma. Testers expressed confusion over whether the app was for finding restaurants or people to go to restaurants with. To address this, our team decided to include an onboarding process to help users understand the app.

Onboarding

Our team created an onboarding experience based on user feedback to accomplish two goals. The first goal is to explain the app's purpose. The second goal is to provide an interactive tutorial for people to perform functions like swiping though it is not required.
