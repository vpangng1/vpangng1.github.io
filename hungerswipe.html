Project Details

My Role: Visual & Interaction Designer

Duration: 7 Months+

Tools: Figma, Illustrator

Background

HungerSwipe is a mobile application that helps friends and family decide on a place to eat more efficiently. HungerSwipe does this by adopting the swipe operation model seen in popular apps like Tinder. As the visual and interaction designer, I was tasked with coming up with designs that would integrate the swipe feature with crowd-sourced local restaurant review & location apps such as Yelp.

Research Insights

Through findings, surveys, and interviews, our user research team discovered that our target users were mostly young, social media app connoissuers. This led to the creation our main persona Sierra Miller, someone who is invested in social media apps and enjoys sharing her experiences with friends. Pain points include feeling overwhelmed with choices and being indecisive when pressured to make group decisions.

Problem

When it comes to deciding where to eat there are factors people have to consider such as maximum travel distance, price range, type of food, etc. Deciding which place to eat with one or more people can be a frustrating experience for everyone because their preferences have to be taken into account.

Solution

We believe implementing a swipe feature that allows users to swipe left or right on desired restaurants makes it easier for parties to decide where to eat. We will know when we're right when we see an increase in downloads, satisfaction, and ratings.
