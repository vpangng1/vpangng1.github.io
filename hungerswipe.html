Project Details

My Role: Visual & Interaction Designer

Duration: 7 Months+

Tools: Figma, Illustrator

Background

HungerSwipe is a mobile application that helps people decide on a place to eat more efficiently. HungerSwipe does this by adopting the swipe operation model seen in popular apps like Tinder. As the visual and interaction designer, I was tasked with coming up with ways to integrate the swipe feature with crowd-sourced local restaurant review & location apps such as Yelp.

Problem

When it comes to choosing a place to eat there are factors that have to be taken into consideration such as maximum travel distance, price range, type of food, etc. Deciding which place to eat with one or more people becomes frustrating and can lead to arguments because their preferences have to be taken into account.

Solution

We believe implementing a swipe feature that allows users to swipe left or right on desired restaurants makes it easier for parties to decide where to eat. We will know when we're right when we see an increase in downloads, satisfaction, and ratings.

Research Insights

Through findings, surveys, and interviews, our user research team discovered that our target users were mostly young, social media app users. This led to the creation our model user Sierra Miller; someone who is invested in social media apps and enjoys sharing her experiences with friends. Her pain points include feeling overwhelmed with choices and being indecisive when pressured to make group decisions.


Challenge: There were concerns that even though both parties find a match on a restaurant, they still may not agree on the spot to eat. In addition, people were confused if the app was for finding restaurants or people to go to restaurants with.


Decision Point: To address potential conflict that may arise while both parties are swiping on possible places to eat, the design team decided to make the process anonymous until a place has been decided. To address the issue of what the app is about, our team decided on designing an on-boarding process.

Design Ideation & Sketches

Early concept sketches of the HungerSwipe app consisted of pen and paper. With Tinder's interface in mind, users would be swiping through restaurants instead of people. This was done to increase simplicity and ease for our target users who are already familiar with Tinder.
