Background

ReliefLink is a homeless resource app which allows homeless people with mobile devices quickly find local resources. As part of General Assembly's hackathon, our team had two days to build a basic but functional application to showcase to judges. As the user researcher, my goal was to conduct research, iterate & test designs, and help facilitate development of the app with developers.

Research

Our team conducted research regarding homeless smart phone ownership and learned that over half a million people experience homelessness every night (as of 2018) and that 62% of homeless youth own a smartphone.

Proto-Persona

James is our proto-persona. He is a twenty-five year old homeless adult who uses his smart phone to search for food, clothing, shelters, and medication. He is wary of unreliable sources and is hesitant to travel to unsafe locations unless he knows more information about it.

Feature Prioritization

Our team brainstormed a list of features for the app that would allow homeless people find reliable locations for resources. This included a search bar, custom filters, resource voting, resource entry & validation, quick category filters, and site info. Due to time constraints, our team focused on developing the quick category filters and site info features as they address our persona's main concerns.

Design Approach

Our team discussed what types of categories were important for quick categories filters and decided on food, shelter, clothing, and medication as they are basic human needs. For the site info feature, our team agreed on creating a map screen similar to Waze where users click on a site of interest and a pop up message would appear displaying information about the location. While the developers on our team started coding the map and site info features, Alexis and I worked on coming up with sketches and designs of the quick filter categories for prototype testing.
