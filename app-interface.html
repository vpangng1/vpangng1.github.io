Introduction

Hazel Analytics is a food tech company specializing in food safety analytical software that they market to restaurants to drive business growth and provide business insights. As part of our client project at General Assembly, I worked as the Interaction Designer tasked with creating an interactive prototype on the iPad platform. Given that we only had 3 weeks, our team, consisting of myself, the Project Manager, and the UX Researcher, focused on the onboarding experience.

Research / User Experience / Visual Design / Interaction Design / Prototype
Problem

Restaurant owners need a simple way to stay prepared and informed about food safety to help them and their staff plan efficiently and effectively for health department inspections.

Solution

Focusing on the default view, customizable dashboard, and the onboarding experience of the app will help restaurant owners better prepare for future health inspections with useful insights whilst also providing them with an overall intuitive experience.

Strategic Analysis

I conducted a comparative analysis on existing apps in the food safety space to discover common features. I discovered that ability to share information, get help, and visualize data. The project manager noted that Hazel Analytics has little to no direct competition because our topic was very niche. However, we believe there was value in taking notes existing features in similar apps to help with feature prioritization.

Research

Restaurant owners were hard to recruit for interviewing as they were most likely dealing with daily operations at the restaurant(s). Our researcher attempted to reach out to these targeted users through Email, Reddit, Slack, Facebook, and Linkedin, but with little results.

Despite our best efforts, our researcher managed to interview one general manager at a pizza place and 5 responses from our survey. Most of the research on our project was based off of past interviews from single and multiple restaurant owners that our client was gracious to provide, given the lack of responses from our target users. We then proceeded to create an affinity diagram. We mapped out things like what restaurant owners felt toward food safety inspection, the technologies they used, whether they cared about competitors, etc.

Design Studio
View Design Studio

We hosted a design studio at the Hazel Analytics headquarters to brainstorm ideas for the visual layout and design of the onboarding process and default screens. I presented some preliminary sketches to the client so the client could get a sense of what they should be drawing. Together we discussed what potential features would be on the default screen. What I got out of this design studio was to have a streamlined onboarding experience and an intuitive default screen that would have some way to view insights, ranking, competitors, filtering, and news.

Usability Testing
View User Testing

We conducted 3 rounds of user testing. We conducted the 1st round of prototpying with paper cutouts and the 2nd and 3rd round with the Figma prototyping tool. In every round, we gave each tester two different task scenarios and two tasks to complete for each.

Lo-fi Wireframes
View Lo-fi Wireframes

After paper prototypes, I began building a low fidelity version of the app in Figma. They covered sign up and sign on, restaurant entry and confirmation screen, single-unit dashboard screen, and adding modules and filtering. The multi-unit side had the dashboard screen and restaurant toggler.
